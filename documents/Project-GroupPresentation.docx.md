This milestone is your opportunity to present and showcase your completed project. Each group will create a 10-to-15-minute video presentation (approximately 5 to 10 minutes per person) that clearly communicates your work, your contributions, and what you’ve learned throughout the project. Your video presentation will be shared with the class during Project Demo Week (Week 13). 

Your video should be clear and well-organized. You may use any recording method that works for your group, such as webcam and microphone, screen capture, PowerPoint recording, or other tools.

Please note that presentations exceeding the 15-minute limit will be penalized, so be sure to plan and rehearse accordingly to stay within the allotted time.

## **Video Presentation Instructions**

Here is what to include in your video presentation:

1. Problem Definition: Clearly define the problem your group addressed. Why is it useful? Why did you choose it?  
2. Background and Starting Point: Briefly explain what has been done by others. Are you building on any existing code or ideas? If so, which ones?  
3. Your Contributions: What have YOU done? Be sure to clearly explain and list each group member’s tasks and explain their contributions in detail.  
4. Please note that simply installing and running open-source code is not sufficient for your project. Your work must include meaningful contributions to demonstrate original effort and understanding. This can be done by adding pre-processing or post-processing steps and testing the model on a new dataset, improving the model’s performance or efficiency, evaluating the model under different conditions or scenarios, etc.  
5. Dataset and Pre-processing: Describe the dataset you used. How did you organize it (e.g., training, test, validation)? Share examples and explain any pre-processing steps and why they were necessary.  
6. Computer Vision Components: Explain the key computer vision techniques used in your implementation, with a focus on your own contributions. Each group member should teach the class something they learned through the project.  
7. Project Demo: Show a live or recorded demo of your project in action.  
8. Results and Evaluation: Share your results, both qualitative and quantitative. If you started with existing code, how did you improve it? How does your solution perform under different conditions and scenarios?  
9. Reflection and Learning: What did you learn from this experience? What was easy, hard, or fun? Would you continue working on this project after the course?
